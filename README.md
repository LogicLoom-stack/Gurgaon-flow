# Gurgaon Flow: Thermal & AQI Data Sculpture (v5.1)

**A Generative Exploration of Urban Heat Islands and Atmospheric Haze**

`Gurgaon Flow` is a volumetric data sculpture that visualizes the urban evolution of Gurugram from 2014 to 2026. Inspired by the works of Refik Anadol, this piece treats the city not as a map, but as a breathing, thermal organism.

### The Narrative
As the viewer moves through the temporal axis (2014–2026), the sculpture evolves. City density is transformed into a **volumetric monolith of light**, where height represents urban expansion and color shifts represent the **Urban Heat Island** effect.

*   **Thermal Resonance:** Dense arterial corridors like NH-48 and Golf Course Road are rendered as "incandescent filaments." As the synthetic thermal index rises, the visual language shifts from cool cyans to fiery oranges and magentas.
*   **Atmospheric Haze:** A real-time particle system responds to fluctuating AQI levels. In years of high pollution, the "data dust" thickens, obscuring the city and creating a sense of environmental claustrophobia.
*   **Kinetic Traffic:** Neon flow particles trace the city’s movement, reacting to both the urban growth and the shifting environmental temperature.

### Technical Stack
*   **Language:** JavaScript / p5.js (WEBGL mode)
*   **Techniques:** Perlin Noise for organic growth, Synthetic Data Mapping, Volumetric Rendering, Orbit Control.
